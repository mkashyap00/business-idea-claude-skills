The 5-Domain Strategic Interrogation Framework
This framework is used as a final strategic safeguard to expose blind spots, overcome optimism bias, and deeply fact-check underlying assumptions.
1. Deep Factual Assumption Validation (The Iron Law)
- Identify all implicit assumptions embedded in the business plan and independently research them before addressing the main strategy.
- Web search summaries are for triage only; any factual claim must be validated by retrieving and reading the full source document.
2. The 5 Domains of Inquiry Apply these five modes of questioning strictly to the proposed plan:
- Investigative (What's Known?): Dig deep to generate nonobvious information and clarify the core purpose. Ask successive "Why?" questions to uncover root causes and challenge whether the team is failing to go deep enough into the actual problem.
- Speculative (What If?): Overcome limiting assumptions and reframe the problem by asking "How might we...?" and "What else could this system allow?".
- Productive (Now What?): Assess the availability of talent, time, and resources. Identify execution bottlenecks and ask if the team genuinely has the capabilities and bandwidth to keep up the pace
- Interpretive (So, What...?): Synthesize the findings and convert information into actionable insight. Ask what was learned, continually redefine the core issue, and ensure the specific plan ladders up to the overarching mission.
- Subjective (What's Unsaid?): Expose the emotional and personal aspects of the decision. Look for hidden agendas and "pluralistic ignorance"—where team members fail to share misgivings because no one else is doing so. Ask whether all stakeholders are genuinely aligned or if there is unspoken frustration.
