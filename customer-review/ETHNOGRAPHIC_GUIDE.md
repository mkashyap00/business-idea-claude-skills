Framework for Validating Customer Pain Points and Reality
This guide ensures product validation is based on actual human behavior rather than assumptions, polite lies, or idealized scenarios.
1. Jobs to Be Done (JTBD) Customers do not buy products; they "hire" them to make specific progress in a given set of circumstances.
- Job Dimensions: Map the functional task, the emotional desire (how the user wants to feel), and the social dimension (how the user wants to be perceived).
- Identify the Actors: Distinguish between the Job Executor (primary user), the Product Lifecycle Support Team (installers/maintainers), and the Buyer (financial decision-maker), especially in B2B environments.
2. The Mom Test (Neutralizing Bias) Most traditional customer feedback fails because people will lie to protect your feelings To extract hard facts:
- Talk about their life, not your idea: Focus on the customer's routines and current workarounds rather than pitching your solution.
- Ask about the past, not the future: Ignore hypotheticals like "Would you use this?" Instead, ask, "Tell me about the last time you faced this problem".
- Identify Real Commitment: Compliments and excitement are false positives. Validation only occurs when a customer has spent actual time, money, or effort trying to solve the problem.
- Embrace Silence: Listen more than you talk and allow awkward pauses; customers will often fill the silence with valuable insights.
3. Bridging the Say-Do Gap
- Naturalistic Observation: Observe users in their actual environment to capture the "Say-Do Gap"—the distance between what users claim they do and how they actually perform tasks.
- Identify Latent Needs: Look for workarounds or struggles that users have become so accustomed to that they no longer articulate them.
